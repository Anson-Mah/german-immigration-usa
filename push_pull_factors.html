What made the USA attractive to German Immigrants?

Antisemitism

Many German Jews left due to antisemitism in Germany. Antisemitism especially saw a massive increase when Hitler rised to power. Hitler's anti-Jewish rhetoric turned the people against the Jews, and Jewish people had to flee in order to not be persecuted.

Freedom of Religion

The right to practice any religion is protected under the U.S. Constitution. German Jews find this useful because they would not be as discriminated for being Jewish as compared to in Germany.

German Revolutions of 1848

Political refugees flee from their homeland upon the failure of the revolutions. The failure of the revolutions showed Germans that political reform in their home country would not be feasible for the foreseeable future.

Freedom of Speech

Freedom of Speech is protected under the U.S. Constitution. Political refugees find this useful because they can voice their political beliefs without having to worry about persecution.

Prussian Famine

There was a massive famine in East Prussia during 1708-1711. Over 250,000 people, accounting for 41% of East Prussia's population, died of starvation and disease during this massive famine.

Lots of Land

The Prussians wanted escape their famine-struck country. Their destination was the United States, as the U.S. had lots of fertile land.
