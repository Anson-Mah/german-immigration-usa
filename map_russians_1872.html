RUSSIAN-GERMANS (1872)

Privileges that were granted to German farmers in Russia were revoked. This caused thousands of German farmers to emigrate from Russia to the United States. These farmers settled out West, particularly in the Dakotas, Nebraska, and Colorado, where more farmland was available.

SETTLEMENT PATTERNS

Throughout all of United States history, Germans have always been immigrating to the nation. German immigration can be categorized into several different “immigration waves”, each with differing characteristics but all share German ancestry.
