CHALLENGES OVERCAME

Nativism

Mass immigration from Ireland and Germany sparked a fear that the current Anglo-Saxon Protestant society of the United States would be taken over by the poor, uneducated, and Catholic immigrants. A new political party, the American Party, or more commonly known as the Know-Nothings, was formed as part of a nativist movement in the 1800s. This political party campaigned for longer naturalization periods, excluding immigrants from holding any public office, and straight up turning away any future Roman Catholic immigrants.

Anti-German Sentiment

The image associated with German identity quickly became negative upon the outbreak of the First World War. Mass Anti-German hysteria arose, and German-Americans were pressured to discard their German identity, accelerating the loss of German culture in the United States. This Anti-German sentiment was further fueled by the Second World War, and German culture in America was nearly eradicated because of it.
