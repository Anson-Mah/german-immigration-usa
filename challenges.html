Religious Discrimination

Germans were often discriminated against due to their alignment with Roman Catholicism. Many people in the U.S. were Protestant, leaving Roman Catholics in the minority of Christians.

Anti-German Sentiment

When the United States joined the First World War, many people started to develop a dislike for Germans. Germans in the United States were pressured to rid themselves of their German roots, to discard their German identity.
