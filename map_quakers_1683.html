QUAKERS (1683)

The first set of Germans came to the New World. Thirteen Quaker families left Germany and headed West, where they found a spot near modern-day Philadelphia and founded Germantown, the first German settlement in the New World.

SETTLEMENT PATTERNS

Throughout all of United States history, Germans have always been immigrating to the nation. German immigration can be categorized into several different “immigration waves”, each with differing characteristics but all share German ancestry.
